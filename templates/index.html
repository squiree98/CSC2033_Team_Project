Climate Action

What is Climate Change?

Climate change is a real and undeniable threat to human existence. It refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. The world is now warming faster than at any point in recorded history.

What are the causes?

Some shifts may be natural but since the 1800s, human activities have been the main driver of climate change, primarily to the burning of fossil fuels.

Human causes include:

  • Generating power by burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas
  • Manufacturing goods that produce emissions from burning fossil fuels
  • Deforestation - cutting down forests causes emissions as trees release the carbon they have been storing
  • Transportation - most cars, lorries and aircraft use fossil fuels
  • Food production - food requires energy to produce whilst cattle release methane, a powerful greenhouse gas

What are the effects?

  • Hotter temperatures - this makes it difficult to work, farm food, preserve water and increases the risk of wild fires - average global temperatures in 2020 were 0.98 degrees celcius warmer than 20th century average and July 2021 was the hottest month ever recorded
  • More severe storms - causing flooding and landslides, destroying homes and farmland
  • Increased drought - water becomes scarcer and deserts expand
  • Rising oceans - as oceans absorb heat and ice sheets melt, they rise causing flooding and destroying low lying land and islands - shrinking ice sheets, data from NASA shows that Greenland lost an average of 279 billion tonnes of ice per year between 1993 and 2019 while Antarctica lost 148 billion tonnes per year
  • Loss of species - climate change poses risks to the survival of many species, as their natural habitat changes or disappears
  • Food shortages - climate change will make it more difficult to farm food in certain areas
  • Health risks - rising temperatures can lead to an increase in diseases and food shortages can lead to poor nutrition
  • Migration - because certain areas will become uninhabitable - 800 million people, 11% of the world's population is currently vulnerable to climate change impacts such as droughts, floods and heatwaves

How can you help

Small lifestyle changes can help such as:

  • Recycling paper, glass, metal, plastic and old electronics
  • Bike, walk or take public transport
  • Eat less meat as the meat production industry has a huge impact on the environment
  • Choose reusable products such as an eco-bag for shopping

Donate to a charity that supports actions to limit climate change such as Clean Air Task Force, Carbon180 and Evergreen Collaborative which can be found here.
